How to survive the pandemic?

Coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) is a contagious disease caused by severe acute respiratory syndrome
coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2). COVID-19 has threatened the lives and livelihoods of everyone on the planet.
To respond, we must take several urgent actions. This is a guide to provide information to help prevent the spread of Covid 19.
Know how coronavirus spreads to protect yourself and others.

Do your part to save the world from Covid 19

Measures to reduce the spread of the virus

Wear a mask

Wearing a mask reduces the spread of Covid 19. Wearing a mask is absolutely essential if you have covid
since it blocks the particles from leaving your nose and infecting others.
Wash your hands with soap

The outermost layer of the virus is made of lipids or fats. Hence they can be broken down by soap. Washing
hands regularly with soap will kill viruses. If you can't wash hands with soap, use an alcohol based hand
sanitizer.
Follow social distancing

If you are near other people keep a minimum distance of 6 feet or 2 metres from them for their safety and yours.
Get the vaccine

The vaccine is the single most effective weapon against the coronavirus. The vaccine does not make you
immune to the coronavirus but even if you get covid it will be mild compared to if you were unvaccinated. It
also reduces the spred of the virus.

FAQ

How does the virus spread
COVID-19 spreads when an infected person breathes out droplets and very small particles that contain the virus.
These droplets and particles can be breathed in by other people or land on their eyes, noses, or mouth.
In some circumstances, they may contaminate surfaces they touch. People who are closer than 6 feet from the infected person are most likely to get infected.
How to protect yourself and others
1. Get vaccinated and stay up-to date on Covid 19 vaccines
2. Wear a mask over your nose and mouth especially if you have Covid
3. Follow social distancing norms and stay 6 feet away from everyone
4. Avoid poorly ventilated spaces and crowds, go outside only if necessary
5. Wash your hands regularly with soap and water or use a sanitizer
Who is at risk of developing severe illness
Although anybody can catch Covid 19, people with pre existing medical conditions such as asthma, lung disease cancer are especially vulnerable.
In addition older people also appear to develop serious illness more often than others.
Is the Covid 19 vaccine safe?
The vaccines approved by WHO are safe to use and if you are eligible you should get both doses and/or boosters as soon as possible.
Before taking vaccine consult with your doctor if you may have an allergic reaction to specific vaccines or not.
